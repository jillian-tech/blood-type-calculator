General Blood Facts

Universal Donor and Recipient

Blood type O-negative is the "universal donor," meaning it can be given to anyone. AB-positive is the "universal recipient," and can receive blood from any type.

The Rarest Type

AB-negative is the rarest blood type, occurring in less than 1% of the population. O-positive is the most common.

Genetic Facts

Why are there different types?

Blood types are determined by the presence or absence of certain antigens on the surface of red blood cells. These antigens can trigger an immune response.

Rh Factor

The Rh factor is another antigen. If you have it, you're Rh-positive ($Rh^+$); if not, you're Rh-negative ($Rh^-$).
